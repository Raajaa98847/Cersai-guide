PART 1

Introduction

The Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest of India (CERSAI) is one of the most important regulatory systems in the Indian banking industry. It acts as a centralized online registry where banks and financial institutions register their security interests over movable and immovable assets.

Its primary objective is to prevent multiple financing against the same property, reduce fraud, improve transparency, and strengthen the secured lending ecosystem.

History of CERSAI

Before CERSAI, there was no centralized database to verify whether a property had already been mortgaged to another bank. This resulted in fraudulent multiple financing.

  • Same property pledged to multiple banks
  • Difficulty in identifying existing mortgages
  • High credit risk
  • Legal disputes during recovery

To address these issues, the Government of India established CERSAI under the SARFAESI Act, 2002. The registry became operational in 2011.

Objectives of CERSAI

  • Prevent multiple financing
  • Increase transparency
  • Protect lenders' rights
  • Reduce fraud
  • Strengthen credit discipline
  • Support digital banking
Important: Every secured lender should register the security interest within the prescribed timeline to ensure regulatory compliance.

Legal Framework

CERSAI operates under the SARFAESI Act, 2002 along with various government notifications and registration rules.

Importance of CERSAI

Example: A borrower owns a property worth ₹1 crore. Without CERSAI: Bank A, Bank B and Bank C may all lend against the same property. With CERSAI: Banks search the registry before sanctioning loans and can identify existing charges.

PART 3

CERSAI Compliance Timelines

Timely registration of security interest is one of the most important compliance requirements for banks and NBFCs. Every institution should complete registration within the regulatory timeline to protect its security interest and comply with internal policies.

  • Monitor pending registrations daily.
  • Track ageing through MIS.
  • Escalate overdue cases immediately.
  • Ensure satisfaction is filed after loan closure.

Operational Challenges

  • Delay in receiving legal documents.
  • Incorrect borrower or property details.
  • Survey number mismatch.
  • Portal downtime.
  • Manual data entry errors.
  • Duplicate registrations.
  • Delay in satisfaction processing.
Note: Most operational issues can be avoided through strong Maker–Checker verification, standard checklists, and quality audits.

Common Audit Observations

  • Delayed CERSAI registration.
  • Incorrect property address.
  • Mismatch between sanction letter and CERSAI data.
  • Pending satisfaction requests.
  • Incomplete document verification.
  • Maker–Checker process not followed.

Risk of Incorrect Registration

Incorrect registration can create legal disputes, delay recovery proceedings, impact customer service, and result in regulatory observations. Therefore, quality checks are essential before final submission.

Best Practices

  • Use a centralized CERSAI operations team.
  • Maintain daily pending MIS.
  • Conduct regular quality audits.
  • Provide refresher training to staff.
  • Automate data transfer from the Loan Management System.
  • Monitor dashboard-based compliance.
  • Maintain maker-checker controls.
Example Workflow Loan Disbursed → Mortgage Created → Documents Verified → Maker Entry → Checker Approval → CERSAI Registration → SI ID Generated → System Updated → Loan Closure → Satisfaction Filing

Conclusion

An effective CERSAI process requires timely registration, accurate data, strong verification controls, and continuous monitoring. Institutions that follow standardized procedures reduce operational risk and improve regulatory compliance while delivering better customer service.

PART 4 - ENTITY MANAGEMENT

Entity Registration

Entity Registration enables eligible organizations to register with the CERSAI portal and access services such as Security Interest Registration, Asset Search, Assignment of Receivables, and Entity Management.

Entity Categories

  • Secured Creditor
  • Asset Reconstruction Company (ARC)
  • Factoring Company
  • Revenue Authority
  • Other Creditor
  • IFSCA Entity

Registration Modes

  • Digital Signature (DSC)
  • CKYC
Note: Entity Registration is available before login in the CERSAI portal.
